Our Story

Born in Crisis

Anubhav became operational when the world came to a standstill due to the COVID-19 outbreak. While the world locked down, we saw the heartbreaking reality in our neighborhoods. People were starving for food. Essential items like sanitary pads, baby food, masks, and sanitizers were unavailable to those who needed them most.

We could not stand by and watch. In response, we launched our first massive relief mission. We carried out two back-to-back projects in the deep interior areas of Sundarbans and Namkhana. We successfully reached out to 100 households, providing them with food rations and essential hygiene kits. That spirit of service in the face of adversity defined who we are today.

Since then, we have expanded our work to Jhargram, Purulia, and Bankura, continuing our mission to support the underprivileged.

Our Projects

From Crisis to Unity: Where it All Began The Sundarbans isn't just a project location for us; it is the birthplace of Anubhav. Our journey began here during the height of the COVID-19 outbreak, when the region was paralyzed by lockdowns. Witnessing 100 households in Namkhana and the deep interior struggle for basic needs, we launched our first-ever relief drives—delivering food, sanitary pads, baby food, and masks when no one else could.

Completed Project

Festive Joy in Peniabhnga

Peniabhnga, Jhargram | In Collab with Mrs. Sapna Midya

On the auspicious eve of Durga Puja, Anubhav traveled to the remote village of Peniabhnga to celebrate with the Sabar tribal community. We believe festivals are for everyone, and our goal was to share the joy of the puja with those often left behind.

We distributed new clothes, learning kits, cakes, and biscuits to nearly 60 children across different age groups, lighting up their faces with smiles. This beautiful initiative was conducted in collaboration with the kind-hearted Mrs. Sapna Midya. To conclude the celebration, we arranged a grand community lunch for nearly 100 people, ensuring the day was filled with food, fun, and togetherness.

Winter Relief in Pabra Village

29th December, 2024

As the year 2024 came to a close, Anubhav extended its reach to Pabra village in the Bankura district to face the harsh winter. The biting cold was making life difficult for many families in the area.

In a humble effort to provide warmth and comfort, we organized a blanket distribution drive. We successfully provided 50 blankets to the most vulnerable beneficiaries. It was our small way of doing whatever we could to help these families battle the shivering cold and sleep peacefully.
